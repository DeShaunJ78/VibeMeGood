Add sharp vs public money tracking to

This shows HOW a line moved not just THAT
it moved. Sharp money moves lines against
public consensus. Public steam moves lines
with consensus.

STEP 1 — Sharp money detection engine
Create artifacts/api-server/src/lib/propedge/sharp-detector.ts

interface SharpSignal {
  marketId: number
  playerName: string
  statType: string
  lineValue: number
  side: "over" | "under"
  publicPct: number
  sharpPct: number
  signal: "sharp" | "public" | "neutral"
  confidence: "low" | "medium" | "high"
  explanation: string
}

Sharp money is identified by REVERSE LINE 
MOVEMENT — when the line moves AGAINST the
public betting percentage.

Example of sharp money:
- 70% of public bets are on OVER
- Line moves TOWARD UNDER (opposite of public)
- This means sharp money is hammering the UNDER
- Sharp signal: UNDER ⚡

Example of public steam:
- 70% of public bets are on OVER  
- Line moves TOWARD OVER (same as public)
- This is just public pressure moving the line
- Weaker signal, lower confidence

STEP 2 — Public betting percentage source
The Odds API does not provide public betting
percentages directly.

Approximate from line movement velocity and
direction using this logic:

Strong sharp signal (high confidence):
- Line moved 3%+ probability in under 30 min
- Movement is AGAINST the expected direction
  (e.g. favorite getting shorter odds when
  most bets should be on them already)
- Movement happened early in the week
  (sharp bettors bet early, public bets late)

Moderate sharp signal (medium confidence):
- Line moved 2-3% probability
- Movement direction is unexpected
- Movement happened mid-week

Public steam (low sharp confidence):
- Line moved with obvious public consensus
- Movement happened close to game time
- Multiple books moved simultaneously
  (books balancing, not sharp action)
