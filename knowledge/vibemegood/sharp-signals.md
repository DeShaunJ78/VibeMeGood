# Sharp Money & Reverse Line Movement

## What is reverse line movement?
Reverse line movement (RLM) happens when a line moves **opposite** to where the public is betting.

Example:
- 70% of books moved the line UP (public hammering OVER)
- But one sharp book moved the line DOWN (toward UNDER)
- The sharp book saw heavy UNDER action from professional bettors
- **Sharp signal: UNDER ⚡ (high confidence)**

This is the most reliable sharp money indicator in pick'em markets.

## Why does sharp money matter?

Sharp bettors (professional or syndicate bettors) have:
- Better models and injury intel than the public
- Access to line shopping across many books
- Historical edge vs. closing line (positive CLV)
- They bet early in the week — the public bets the day of the game

When sharps and public disagree, **sharps are right more often**.

## Confidence levels

**High confidence ⚡⚡⚡:**
- Large move (0.5+ line units) against public consensus
- Quick move in the last 30 minutes
- Public 65%+ on one side
- Not all books moving simultaneously (not just balancing)

**Medium confidence ⚡⚡:**
- 0.25–0.5 unit move against consensus
- Public 60%+ on dominant side

**Low confidence ⚡:**
- Small reverse move
- Watch for follow-up confirmation

## Public steam vs. sharp action

| Signal | Meaning | Confidence |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| ⚡ Sharp | Line moved AGAINST public % | High/Medium |
| 📊 Public | Line moved WITH public % | Low — treat with caution |
| — Neutral | Mixed or insufficient data | N/A |

Public steam often fades at closing. Sharp money tends to hold value into game time.

## How to combine with model edge

**Best scenario (fade the public with the model):**
- Model shows UNDER edge (P(Over) < 50%)
- Sharp signal: UNDER (reverse line movement)
- Public betting OVER (crowd going wrong way)
- **All three aligned = highest conviction play**

**Proceed with caution:**
- Model edge exists but no sharp signal → rely on model only
- Sharp signal exists but model neutral → smaller position

**Avoid:**
- Sharp on OVER, model on UNDER → conflicting signals, skip or wait
- Public steam only → weakest signal, no edge

## When NOT to trust sharp signals

- Single move data point (need 2+ moves to detect consensus)
- All books moved together simultaneously (books balancing action, not sharp)
- Move happened close to game time (public bets late, not sharps)
- Very small move size (< 0.25 line units) — noise, not signal

## In this tool

Sharp signals appear in the Slate Board as ⚡ badges on prop rows.
Use the **Sharp Only** filter (⚡ button in the toolbar) to see only props with detected sharp action.
In PropDetailSheet, the **Line Shopping** and **Sharp Signal** panels show the full explanation.

Run **Sync Sharp Signals** in Settings to re-compute all signals against the latest line movement data.
